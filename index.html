01 — About
Dr. Enamul Islam Saad

Clinician. Researcher.

I am a Bangladeshi medical doctor (MBBS) trained at Harbin Medical University, China — one of Asia's leading research institutions. My six-year programme included a rigorous one-year rotating internship at the First Affiliated Hospital of HMU, a 2,500+ bed tertiary care centre.

Currently, I am pursuing a Master of Public Health (MPH) in Epidemiology at Northern University Bangladesh, positioning my clinical foundation within a broader framework of population health, disease surveillance, and health systems research.

My community engagement spans four-plus years with the Bangladesh Red Crescent Society, COVID-19 vaccination campaigns, and active coordination of medical camps through the Neurology Foundation of Bangladesh. I hold over 19 certifications from WHO, UNICEF, USAID, Johns Hopkins, Imperial College London, and Harvard Medical School.

02 — Research Focus

Areas of Inquiry & Expertise

My research interests sit at the intersection of epidemiology, community health systems, and clinical medicine — shaped by field experience and formal academic training.

🦠
Infectious Disease Epidemiology

Disease surveillance, outbreak investigation, and control — particularly infectious diseases with high burden in low-to-middle income countries including COVID-19 and tuberculosis.

❤️
Non-Communicable Disease (NCD) Prevention

Preventive nutrition strategies and lifestyle interventions for chronic NCDs. Research in dietary risk factors and their epidemiological linkage to cardiovascular and metabolic disease.

🌍
Global & Community Health Systems

Health program evaluation, community-based family planning, and design of resilient public health emergency response systems for resource-limited settings.

📱
Digital & mHealth Innovation

Mobile health technology for community outreach, health communication effectiveness, and digital tools for disease monitoring and patient management.

🧒
Child & Adolescent Health

Child protection systems strengthening, child safeguarding frameworks, and youth-participatory decision-making models in humanitarian and development contexts.

📊
Epidemiological Methods & Evaluation

Impact evaluation of health communication programmes, measuring disease burden, and applying epidemiological methods to assess public health intervention outcomes.
